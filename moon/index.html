The moral of our story is: A billion acts of courage can spark a brighter tomorrow.

The Story Team at Greenpeace International isn’t just promoting good yarns, or supporting our teams in developing the most compelling narratives about their campaigns. We see story as a theory of change; we are provocateurs, challenging who we are as an organisation, how we perceive ourselves, and how we want others to perceive us. This page is a portal into the work we do to spark courageous acts at Greenpeace.

One of our mantras and goals is 'Courage, not perfectionism'. We're undertaking an experiment here where we publish more early and more often, in the name of greater openness, so some material you'll encounter may be unfinished and unpolished at the moment. This is where you come in: talk to us, tell us what struck you, what rabbit holes you were led to, what's missing, how to move it to wowza. Help us see the possibilities you see.

Our Audience

Whether staff, alumni or volunteer, our audience is people who are already passionate about making the world a more green and peaceful place. This document is the high-level view on how we engage with our audience.

Community Call

As part of our commitment to becoming a more open organization, we run biweekly community calls. A community call is a recurring call which is open to anyone who wants to join. You might think of them as office hours for particular themes or design sessions for programs. Next call date/time and dial in instructions are here.

Courage is...

Did you know that the word, “courage” used to mean something entirely different than it does today? Courage Is... seeks to define kinds of courage: physical, moral, intellectual, social, emotional and spiritual courage. Courage is the cultural currency of the brighter tomorrow.
