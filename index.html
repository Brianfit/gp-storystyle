What “Our Story” Means to Greenpeace.

Context

A story can mean many things. It can be a compelling narrative about real events that have occurred. It can be an imagined vision of what’s possible. It can be an illustration of worldview expressed by a storyteller with the purpose of rallying support. Our definition includes all of these things, but it is more expansive.

Commitment

No single communication and no single activity fully expresses our story. Rather, our story comes to life in how the world experiences our words, our deeds and our brand over time. No single individual is our master storyteller. Everyone who works on our behalf will now be empowered to contribute to the global story we tell.

Catalyze

By seeing our brand as an unfolding story, we will become more adept at storytelling as it is more commonly defined. So this Brand Guide offers strategic insights to align our words and deeds, as well as actionable tools for turning fact-based communications into compelling narratives that spur action and spread our worldview beyond “the choir” we now each.

This is our story. And we are now all storytellers.

With the publication of this document, Greenpeace is committing to the idea that the sum total of all of our communications and our actions will add up to a coherent, ongoing story that unfolds between our organization and those we inspire to act in collaboration with us.

We will tell our story — and live it — every day until we have achieved a more green and peaceful world.

Our Internal Brand

Read and absorb. You won’t find action items here, just the deep thinking behind our story-based brand.

The Heroes

Courageous Citizens Everywhere

The world we aspire to will not be created by a single organization or by a handful of professional activists. Change depends on the individual and coordinated actions of millions. The true heroes of our story are the citizens we inspire to action.

Greenpeace Activists

Catalyzing courageous action takes role models willing to put themselves on the line on behalf of the planet. Greenpeace activists are the “heroes among heroes.” They are role models who fortify people’s belief that one courageous act can be contagious and that people really can change the world.

The Gift

The Keys to Courage

We’re going to teach people how to grow courage within themselves and practice it in the real world. Greenpeace’s research into the value of courage reveals that it is one of the most desired of all personal attributes and is universally recognized as a positive trait.

Our Archetype

Champions of the Impossible

From our original adventure into a nuclear test zone that rocked the world’s consciousness to our takedowns of corporate polluters, we’ve never believed that humans are inherently bad or that one person’s actions can’t spark a million more.

Moral

A billion acts of courage will change the world

From reusing a shopping bag to publicly taking a stance against toxins in our clothing – every act that reduces waste and protects our planet is an act of courage.

Values

Courage, Connectivity, Hope

Successful brands and organizations evolve from a set of core values that drive and prioritize activities. These values guide internal culture and external relationships, providing access points for our audiences based on values they share with us.

On the surface of our story, we find our public communications and actions. These are the visible elements of the stories we tell. This map defines the heroes of these stories, the gift we give to empower our heroes and the human voice of our organization (our archetype). One layer below the surface, we find the moral of our story. This is the core truth that unites everything we say and do. That moral, or worldview, is based in turn on values we seek to share with our audiences and live out in our organization.

Myth Gaps & Our Vision

We need new myths.

The world we live in today is built on stories — stories that support the status quo. As people become increasingly aware of the problems we face, these stories are making less and less sense. Addressing these “myths” head on, and offering new, more compelling ones will make our brand far more appealing and culturally relevant.
The crumbling myths you’ll find in this section define our view of the “broken world.” Our job, however, is no longer only to yell about how broken the world is. We must also create the myths of our healed world, because myths — or meaning stories — have always guided human behavior. Without them we will never create a thriving future. Take heart; these new myths are not ours to build alone . They are already emerging everywhere. We will speed them along.
